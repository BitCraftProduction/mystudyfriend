Top Government Jobs Sectors After Arts Subject with Salary

1. Civil Services: The Big League Jobs

Have you ever heard about civil service jobs? These include jobs like the IAS, IPS, IFS or IRS? These are top-tier government jobs that come with a lot of respect from citizen. People working in these jobs help run the country well and tackle the social issues. They can earn between ₹56,100 to ₹2,50,000 per month depending on their ranks and experience.

2. Teaching: Shaping Young Minds

If you love sharing knowledge and teaching children, whether children are from primary schools, high schools, or colleges. A teacher plays a crucial role in shaping their future. In India, there are many government jobs available for teaching at various levels. Depending on where they teach and their qualifications, they can earn anywhere from ₹20,000 to ₹90,000 per month.

3. Heritage and Culture: Keeping Traditions Alive

Many people are fascinated by history and culture. There are cool jobs in archaeology, museums, libraries, and cultural departments where you can work as a government employee, and you can preserve and showcase India's rich heritage. These jobs pay around ₹25,000 to ₹60,000 per month. Whatever you say it's an exciting job.

4. Communication: Connecting with People

If you're good with words and enjoy talking with different people, you might like jobs in public relations. Public relations officers help government agencies communicate better with the public in several scenarios. They earn about ₹30,000 to ₹60,000 per month.

These are the top government jobs sectors for arts students. No matter what your interests are, there's always any government job out there! Whether you love shaping policies, teaching students, preserving history or talking to public.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Which job is best for arts student?

There isn't a single "best" job for arts students. It totally depends on individual interests, skills, and career goals. Teaching, civil services, cultural preservation, and communication roles are popular options for arts students.

2. Which government exam is best for arts students?
The best government exam for arts students depends on their interests and career aspirations. Government exams such as the Civil Services Examination (including IAS, IPS, IFS), SSC Combined Graduate Level (CGL), and State Public Service Commission exams are popular exams pursued by arts graduates.

3. Which arts job has the highest salary?
Civil services positions such as the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), and Indian Foreign Service (IFS) typically offer some of the highest salaries for arts graduates, with entry-level salaries ranging from ₹56,100 to ₹2,50,000 per month.

4. Which subject is best for a government job?
There isn't a single "best" subject for a government job as it depends on the specific job role and department. However, subjects like history, political science, economics, and public administration are commonly associated with government jobs due to their relevance to civil services and administrative roles.
